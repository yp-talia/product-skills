## Key Findings

1. **Strongest claims:** The Amicable/Octopus acquisition (Claim 1), Wenup prenup pricing (Claim 3), and Spring 2026 cohabitation consultation (Claim 7) are all robustly verified across 3-5+ independent sources.

2. **Rejected — Wenup cohabitation "coming soon" (Claim 4):** This is the most significant error. Cohabitation agreements are now a live, priced product on Wenup's platform (GBP 499.50 per person). This undermines the competitive analysis framing in the example, which presents the cohabitation space as having only an imminent future competitor rather than a current one.

3. **Disputed founding date (Claim 2):** "Founded in 2020" appears in PitchBook but is contradicted by LinkedIn (2022) and Companies House incorporation (February 2023). The discrepancy likely reflects project inception vs. company formation, but the claim as stated is misleading.

4. **Uncertain DivorceIQ pricing (Claim 5):** The GBP 97 figure may be accurate for the standard Essentials tier but cannot be confirmed from the currently live website, which shows GBP 49 as an apparent promotional price.

5. **Loose characterisation of STAI-6 (Claim 8):** Calling the STAI-6 a "validated emotional measurement" instrument is technically defensible but imprecise. It is specifically an anxiety measurement tool.

6. **No sources cited in the document:** All claims in EXAMPLE.md are presented as authoritative market intelligence without any attribution, making independent verification unnecessarily difficult.
