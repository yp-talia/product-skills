## Company Overview

**Wise** is a London-based fintech that provides international money transfer and multi-currency banking services. Founded in 2011 by Kristo Käärmann and Taavet Hinrikus (former Skype director of strategy), the company went public on the London Stock Exchange via direct listing in July 2021 and is now a public limited company with company number 13211214 registered at 1st Floor Worship Square, 65 Clifton Street, London, EC2A 4JE. [Source: Companies House, https://find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk/company/13211214, accessed 2026-04-09]

The company operates three business lines: personal money transfers and multi-currency accounts, business payment solutions, and a B2B platform for banks and financial institutions to embed Wise technology. [Source: Wise, https://wise.com, accessed 2026-04-09]

---

## Growth & Scale

### Customer & Volume Metrics

Wise serves **15.6 million active customers** with **£145.2 billion in cross-border volume** annually as of FY2025, up from 12.8 million customers in FY2024. The mobile app has achieved **60 million downloads**. [Source: Wise Investor Relations, https://wise.com/owners/, accessed 2026-04-09]

In H1 FY26 (half-year ending 30 September 2025), active customers grew **18% year-on-year to 13.4 million**. Cross-border volume for the half-year reached **£84.9 billion**. [Source: Wise H1 FY26 Trading Update, https://www.fxcintel.com/research/analysis/wise-h1-26-earnings, accessed 2026-04-09]

Customer holdings (cash and investment balances) grew substantially to **£21.5 billion in FY2025**, up from £16 billion in FY2024. By September 2025, this figure had risen to **£25.3 billion**. [Source: Wise Investor Relations, https://wise.com/owners/, accessed 2026-04-09]

### Financial Performance

Revenue grew **15% to £1.2 billion in FY2025** from £1.1 billion in FY2024. Profit before tax increased **17% to £564.8 million**. [Source: Wise plc Financial Results, https://wise.com/owners/results-reports-presentations, accessed 2026-04-09]

H1 FY26 underlying revenue (excluding one-time items) rose **12% year-on-year to £658 million**. Underlying profit before tax margin reached **16.3%**, placing Wise at the upper end of its medium-term guidance of 13-16%. [Source: Wise H1 FY26 Results, accessed 2026-04-09]

Cross-border revenue, historically the dominant revenue stream, grew only **5% to £441 million**, falling from 71% of total revenue in H1 FY25 to 67% in H1 FY26, signaling successful revenue diversification. Card and other services revenue grew **26% to £217.1 million**. [Source: Wise H1 FY26 Results, accessed 2026-04-09]

### Market Valuation

Wise plc trades on the London Stock Exchange under ticker WISE.L. As of April 7, 2026, the market cap stood at **£9.5 billion**, with the stock trading around **918 GBP per share**. [Source: Financial data aggregators, https://www.investing.com/pro/LSE:WISE/explorer/marketcap, accessed 2026-04-09]

## Strategic Direction & Expansion

### Product Expansion Beyond Transfers

On March 30, 2026, Wise launched a **UK Current Account**, marking a strategic shift into everyday retail banking. The account offers a **3.26% variable interest rate** on GBP balances, integrates Airport Lounge Pass benefits, and includes Young Explorer debit cards for under-18s. [Source: Wise Newsroom, https://newsroom.wise.com/en-UKI/263844-wise-launches-uk-current-account-to-take-on-high-street-banks/, accessed 2026-04-09]

This launch addresses a key strategic insight: non-cross-border revenue already accounts for **41% of underlying income** as of H1 FY26, demonstrating the maturity of Wise's new revenue streams and the company's pivot toward becoming a primary banking provider rather than a niche transfer service. [Source: Wise H1 FY26 Results, accessed 2026-04-09]

### Geographic Expansion

In March 2026, Wise became **"the first non-bank to be granted five licences in Thailand"** for payments, electronic money, foreign exchange, and other services—a milestone that signals growing regulatory acceptance of fintech in Asia. [Source: Wise Newsroom, https://newsroom.wise.com/en-UKI/, accessed 2026-04-09]

In December 2025, Wise received **conditional regulatory approval for its first African license in South Africa**. [Source: Wise Newsroom, accessed 2026-04-09]

In September 2025, Wise expanded its Austin, Texas office hub by **200%, reaching 90,000 square feet across three floors**, signaling significant North American investment. [Source: Wise Newsroom, accessed 2026-04-09]

### Headcount Growth

Wise plans to add **approximately 700 roles in FY2026**. [Source: Wise Investor Relations, accessed 2026-04-09]

## Customer Sentiment

### Trustpilot Rating

Wise holds a **4.3 out of 5 star rating on Trustpilot based on 286,852 customer reviews**, with **80% giving five stars**. [Source: Trustpilot, https://uk.trustpilot.com/review/wise.com, accessed 2026-04-09]

However, a notable minority express serious concerns: **9% of reviewers gave one star**, citing account suspensions without explanation, delayed verification processes, and unresponsive customer support. [Source: Trustpilot, accessed 2026-04-09]

[Inference based on: Trustpilot distribution data] The stark split between highly satisfied (80%) and dissatisfied (9%) customers suggests potential operational friction around account verification and compliance procedures, likely driven by increasing regulatory scrutiny and anti-money laundering requirements.

### App Store Sentiment

On iOS and Android app stores, users consistently praise Wise's **transparent pricing, competitive exchange rates, and speed** (with over 70% of transfers arriving in under 20 seconds). [Source: Wise website, https://wise.com, accessed 2026-04-09] Common complaints include account blocking without clear explanations and variability in transfer speed for certain destinations. [Source: Mobile App Daily, https://www.mobileappdaily.com/product-review/wise-app, accessed 2026-04-09]

## Employee Sentiment

Wise has a **3.8 out of 5 star rating on Glassdoor based on 2,393 anonymous reviews**. [Source: Glassdoor, https://www.glassdoor.com/Reviews/Wise-Reviews-E637715.htm, accessed 2026-04-09]

**70% of employees would recommend Wise to a friend**, and **65% believe the company has a positive business outlook**. [Source: Glassdoor, accessed 2026-04-09] Software engineers rate the company higher at **4.0 out of 5 stars** based on 101 reviews, suggesting stronger satisfaction in technical roles. [Source: Glassdoor, accessed 2026-04-09]

**48% of job candidates rate their interview experience as positive**, with an average difficulty score of 3 out of 5. [Source: Glassdoor, accessed 2026-04-09]

[Inference based on: Glassdoor distribution] A 3.8/5 employee rating alongside generally positive outlook sentiment suggests a company navigating typical profitable-growth fintech trade-offs: strong mission alignment and compensation, offset by occasional process friction and operational strain during geographic expansion.

## Regulatory & Compliance

Wise Payments Limited holds an **electronic money institution (EMI) license from the UK Financial Conduct Authority** (FCA firm reference 900507) under the Electronic Money Regulations 2011 and the Payment Services Regulations 2017. [Source: Wise Help Centre, https://wise.com/help/articles/2932693/how-is-wise-regulated-in-each-country-and-region, accessed 2026-04-09] Wise Assets Limited is separately authorized for investment activities (FCA reference 839689). [Source: Wise, accessed 2026-04-09]

An EMI license allows Wise to issue electronic money and provide payment services but falls short of full banking regulation, which explains why Wise must partner with banks in certain markets and why deposit protection under the FSCS (for UK balances) is limited compared to traditional banks.

## Technology & Platform

Wise operates **over 1,000 microservices** across its backend infrastructure, primarily built in **Java and Kotlin**, with networking centralized using **AWS Transit Gateways**. [Source: Wise Engineering (Medium), https://medium.com/wise-engineering/wise-tech-stack-2025-update-d0e63fe718c7, accessed 2026-04-09]

The Wise Platform API is a REST-based interface enabling integration by businesses. The company does **not use blockchain technology** as part of its core payments infrastructure. [Source: Wise Platform Docs, https://docs.wise.com/, accessed 2026-04-09]

## Competitive Position

In the international money transfer market, Wise is differentiated primarily by **transparent, mid-market exchange rates without markups** and low upfront fees (starting at 0.41%). [Source: Wise blog, https://wise.com/us/blog/, accessed 2026-04-09]

Key competitors include:

- **Revolut**: UK fintech with a broader digital banking product suite; charges between $4–$6 per international transfer on the free tier or includes free transfers in premium tiers. [Source: Wise blog comparison, https://wise.com/us/blog/revolut-vs-wise, accessed 2026-04-09]
- **Remitly**: US-based specialist in remittances to emerging markets; markups range 1–3.7% above mid-market rates. [Source: Wise blog comparison, https://wise.com/us/blog/remitly-vs-wise, accessed 2026-04-09]
- **Western Union**: Legacy provider with 500,000+ agent locations; markups typically 2–4%; increasingly losing share to fintech. [Source: Wise blog comparison, https://wise.com/us/blog/remitly-vs-western-union, accessed 2026-04-09]

## Multi-Perspective Synthesis & Verification

### What Wise Says About Itself
Wise positions itself as enabling "money without borders," emphasizing transparent pricing, speed, and expanding beyond transfers into full banking. CEO Kristo Käärmann's vision is "international financial transactions as seamless as sending an email." [Source: Wise newsroom, accessed 2026-04-09] Recent announcements stress geographic expansion (Thailand, South Africa, North America), product innovation (UK current account, interest-bearing accounts), and social impact (carbon removal budget, IRC humanitarian funding).

### What Investors See
Investors have valued Wise at £9.5 billion market cap (April 2026), up from the £11 billion IPO valuation in July 2021, reflecting modest growth but strong profitability. The company achieved 16.3% underlying profit margins in H1 FY26, placing it at the upper end of guidance, which signals operational discipline. Wise's revenue diversification away from cross-border fees (now 67% vs 71% two years ago) is viewed positively as a de-risking move. At these valuations, Wise trades at a price-to-sales multiple roughly 7-8x, typical for mature fintech with proven margins rather than hypergrowth. [Source: Financial data, accessed 2026-04-09]

### What Employees Say
Employees give Wise a solid 3.8/5 rating with 70% recommending it, indicating authentic buy-in to mission and compensation. Software engineers rate higher (4.0/5), suggesting technical hiring is competitive. The profile suggests a profitable, stable employer without the "rockstar intensity" of venture-backed hypergrowth. [Source: Glassdoor, accessed 2026-04-09]

### What Customers Say
**Misalignment alert**: Customers split sharply between enthusiasts (80% five-star, praising speed and rates) and a persistent unhappy minority (9% one-star, citing account suspensions and poor support). This divergence is more pronounced than in employee sentiment and suggests Wise's aggressive compliance posture (7 million fraud checks daily) may be creating friction for edge-case users or those in high-risk geographies. Unlike employees, customers have little visibility into the business rationale for account suspensions. [Source: Trustpilot, Wise website, accessed 2026-04-09]

### What Press Observes
Recent coverage emphasizes Wise's geographic ambitions (Thailand, South Africa, Austin expansion) and product evolution (UK current account). Press does not report major product controversies or regulatory enforcement actions by FCA or other authorities, suggesting Wise is navigating the compliance landscape effectively. The company is receiving public recognition for social impact (doubled IRC commitment to £1 million in January 2026). Coverage is generally neutral-to-positive; no narrative of decline or crisis. [Source: Wise Newsroom, accessed 2026-04-09]

## Information Gaps & Limitations

1. **User acquisition cost & LTV metrics**: Publicly disclosed financial data does not break down customer acquisition cost or lifetime value by cohort, limiting visibility into unit economics or retention durability.
2. **Churn rate**: Wise does not publicly disclose monthly or annual customer churn, making it difficult to assess whether the 9% one-star Trustpilot cohort represents a small, tolerable segment or a growing problem.
3. **Regulatory incidents**: No major enforcement actions by FCA or other regulators are publicly reported, but smaller compliance concerns or fines may exist below public visibility thresholds.
4. **Competitor head-to-head win rates**: Wise does not disclose how often it wins or loses deals against Revolut, Remitly, or traditional banks in specific corridors.
5. **SMB vs. Consumer mix**: Wise discloses total customer numbers and business growth rates but not the split in revenue or headcount between personal and business segments.

## Key Findings Summary

1. **Profitable and stable**: £1.2 billion revenue, 15.6 million customers, £9.5 billion market cap, and 16.3% profit margins position Wise as a mature, cash-generative fintech leader—not a hypergrowth story.
2. **Successful diversification underway**: Non-transfer revenue now accounts for 41% of income (up from near-zero five years ago), validating the pivot from niche service to multi-product banking platform.
3. **Geographic expansion accelerating**: New licenses in Thailand, South Africa, and expanded US presence signal global ambitions, though execution risk remains in each new market.
4. **Customer satisfaction paradox**: 80% five-star Trustpilot ratings coexist with 9% one-star detractors—likely driven by compliance friction (KYC, AML) in account verification, not product quality. This is a known fintech trade-off between safety and user experience.
5. **Employee confidence solid but mature**: 3.8/5 Glassdoor rating reflects a healthy, profitable growth-stage company rather than venture-backed excitement; typical for a profitable UK fintech with established markets.
6. **Regulatory acceptance growing**: Wise's ability to secure licenses in Asia, Africa, and Europe indicates improving fintech regulatory environment and Wise's reputation as a compliant operator—important for a money services business.
