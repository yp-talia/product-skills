# Output Templates

Suggested output structures for each research skill. Use these directly or adapt to your preferred format (Notion, markdown, Google Docs, etc.).

---

## Company Intelligence

```
# Company Intelligence — {Company Name}

## What They Do
{2-3 sentence overview}

## Founding Story
{Brief history}

## Mission & Values
{What the company says about itself — Tier 1, treated as true}

## Business Model
{How they make money, revenue streams}

## Growth Model & Distribution
{How they acquire customers — PLG, sales-led, partnerships, content, referrals}
{Evidence from job postings, marketing channels, customer acquisition signals}

## Technology Landscape
{Tech stack from job postings, engineering blog, StackShare, GitHub}
{Engineering culture signals}
{How technology enables or constrains product decisions}

## Funding & Financial Health
{Funding stage, last round, investors, revenue indicators, headcount trends}
{Sources: Crunchbase, Companies House, press, LinkedIn}

## Key Milestones
{Timeline of significant events}

## Recent News
{Last 12 months, with links}

## Multi-Perspective Analysis

### What They Say About Themselves
{Website, blog, social media — Tier 1}

### What Investors Say
{Portfolio mentions, funding commentary — Tier 2}

### What Employees Say
{Glassdoor, LinkedIn posts — Tier 3}

### What Customers Say
{Which?, Google Reviews, reviews.io, Trustpilot, G2, app stores — Tier 3}

### What Press Says
{News coverage — Tier 2}

### Misalignment Flags
{Where perspectives diverge — high-value signals}
```

## Product Analysis

```
# Product Analysis — {Company Name}

## Product Overview
{What the product is, key features, how it works}

## Users, Buyers & Beneficiaries
{Who uses it, who pays, who benefits — may be different people}

## Value Chain
{How value flows from creation to delivery to capture}

## Pricing & Revenue Model
{How they charge, price points, tiers}

## Unit Economics Assessment
{CAC signals, LTV signals, gross margin indicators}
{State all assumptions explicitly}
{Flag where data is insufficient}

## Product Maturity
{0→1 / scaling / optimising — with evidence}

## Market Sizing
{TAM / SAM / SOM with methodology stated}

## Growth vs Capture
{Expanding the market or taking share?}

## Product Experience Teardown
{Walkthrough of key user journeys — sign-up, onboarding, core workflow}
{3-5 specific observations with improvement hypotheses}
{If product not publicly accessible, mapped from demos/screenshots/reviews}

## Partnerships & Ecosystem
{Integrations, channel partners, strategic alliances}
{How ecosystem affects product: build vs integrate, constraints, leverage}
```

## Market Landscape

```
# Market Landscape — {Company Name}'s Market

## Porter's Five Forces

### Threat of New Entrants
{Barriers to entry, capital requirements, regulatory moats}

### Bargaining Power of Suppliers
{Key dependencies, switching costs}

### Bargaining Power of Buyers
{Concentration, switching costs, price sensitivity}

### Threat of Substitutes
{Alternative solutions, adjacent markets}

### Competitive Rivalry
{Number and strength of competitors, market growth rate}

## Legislation & Regulation
{Upcoming and recent changes — UK focus, then EU/global}
{Implementation timelines}
{Specific acts/regulations with dates}

## Market Trends
{Technology trends, customer expectation shifts, distribution model changes}

## Demand Signal Classification
{Demand capture / demand generation / latent demand — with evidence}

## Macro Forces
{Economic conditions, investment climate for this sector}
```

## Competitor Analysis

```
# Competitor Analysis — {Company Name}'s Competitive Landscape

## Competitor Map

### Direct Competitors
{Same product category, same customer segment}

### Strategic Competitors
{Different approach to the same problem}

### Adjacent Competitors
{Could expand into this space}

## Feature Comparison Matrix
{Table comparing key features across direct competitors}
| Feature | {Company} | Competitor A | Competitor B | ... |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------------|-----|

## Audience & Segmentation
{Who each competitor is optimised for}

## Distribution & Marketing
{PLG vs sales-led, content strategy, partnerships}

## Pricing & Revenue Comparison
| Competitor | Pricing Model | Entry Price | Enterprise | Revenue Signals |
|-----------|--------------|------------|-----------|----------------|

## Brand Perception & Trust
{Review scores, social proof, case studies}
{Sources: Which?, Google Reviews, reviews.io, Trustpilot, G2}

## Underserved Segments
{Customer groups no competitor serves well — opportunities}
```
